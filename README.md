# Multivariate Forecasting and Predictability Analysis of Cryptocurrency Market Dynamics

## Project Background and scope
Cryptocurrency markets have emerged as an important real-world testbed for time-series
modelling due to their high volatility, non-stationary behaviour, and rapidly changing
market regimes. Unlike traditional physical or economic systems, cryptocurrency markets
exhibit strong uncertainty, regime shifts, and complex interactions between price
movement, market activity, and risk dynamics. These characteristics make cryptocurrency
data a valuable domain for studying the limits and capabilities of modern machine learning
and deep learning methods in time-series forecasting.

This capstone project focuses on multivariate time-series forecasting and predictability
analysis in cryptocurrency markets. Rather than aiming to achieve guaranteed or profitable
price prediction, the project investigates which market variables are more or less
predictable, under what conditions, and why.

Using major cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Ethereum as case studies, students will
work with historical market data including price, return, volatility, and trading volume.
Students will develop and compare classical statistical models, machine learning methods,
and deep learning architectures to forecast different market variables over short horizons.
A key emphasis of the project is critical evaluation: students are expected to analyse model
performance across different targets, identify failure modes under non-stationarity and
regime changes, and discuss the limitations of predictive modelling in highly uncertain
environments.

## Project Tasks
- Acquire and clean historical cryptocurrency market data from public data sources
- Perform exploratory data analysis and time-series preprocessing.
- Construct datasets involving price, return, volatility, and trading volume
- Develop statistical, machine learning and deep learning models for multivariate time-
series forecasting
- Evaluate models using appropriate quantitative metrics and compare performance
across different prediction targets
- Critically examine model robustness, regime dependence, and limitations under non-
stationary market conditions

## Expected outcomes
- A set of forecasting models for cryptocurrency market variables (price, return, volatility,
and volume)
- A systematic comparison of univariate and multivariate forecasting approaches across
different prediction targets
- An empirical analysis identifying which aspects of cryptocurrency market behaviour are
more predictable and which are dominated by uncertainty
- A comprehensive technical report detailing the methodology, experimental results,
evaluation, and critical discussion of limitations
